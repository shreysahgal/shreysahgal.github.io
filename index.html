About Me

I'm an incoming freshman at the University of Michigan's College of Engineering where I intend to study computer science and physics. I recently graduated from the Horace Mann School in the Bronx, NY. I'm a big fan of programming and in particular web-app development and data analysis. I've held a number of internships including a year as a fullstack intern at QuiO Technologies, a biotech startup where I got to learn a lot of about what real-world web-dev is all about and a Automation Engineering/Data Analysis internship at Essen Medical Associates, a healthcare company based out of the Bronx.

Outside of the classroom, I co-captained my schools 3 FTC Robotics teams, ran a Horace Mann's annual hackathon, HackMann, for 2 years (this year we pulled in over 150 people!), and competed in a number of hackathons with my friends. I also enjoy reading, playing video games (my genji play is particularly clean--don't feel the need to fact-check that), and thinking about doing things then not doing them.

Projects
Staycate
This is a fun little web app that I made with my friends for Hackcation 2020. The idea is that it allows users to travel to different places vicariously through social media. When you go to somewhere on the Staycate map, the map is populated with social media pictures of nearby attractions/locations.
DancingOrFalling
This is a very silly web game that I made after one of my friends paused some youtube video at a weird moment and I really couldn't tell without context whether the person was mid-fall or breakdancing (turns out it was breakdancing). The game is modeled after Llama or Duck, a game I remember playing in elementary school but I have yet to find someone else who remembers it (maybe it was just a figment of my overactive imagination? If you have played Llama or Duck please contact me!).
VoteSys
This is a nifty utility that I made as a (sort of) final project for a discrete math/voting theory class that I took in my senior year of high school. The idea is that it can take in a CSV of a bunch of votes and spit out a preference schedule and analyze the votes using a number of different techniques (you can find more information about the election theory behind this here). This project let me learn the pain of file i/o and the weirdness that arises when using cookies extensively.
Taylor Poly Visualizer
This is a relatively boring but still fun-to-make visualizer of Taylor Polynomials. I made it with a few friends for a calc project. My friends did most of the math and I got to play around with the Desmos API and funky styling. Certainly not the most intricate project I've made but I still think it's a cool visualization of a neat math concept.
